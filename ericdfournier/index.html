  • Undergraduate Education

    Environmental Science

    In 2008 I received a Bachelor of Science degree from Bucknell University in Lewisburg, PA. While at Bucknell, I worked for three years as an undergraduate researcher in environmental chemistry under Dr. Molly McGuire studying redox reactions involved in the remediation of groundwater pollutants.

  • Graduate Education

    Spatial Environmental Modeling

    In 2010 I graduated from the Yale School of Forestry and Environmental Science with a Master of Science Degree, specializing in the development and application of environmental decision support systems. While at Yale I worked under Dr. Dana Tomlin, credited as the father of Map Algebra.

  • Doctoral Education

    Geoinformatics

    I am currently enrolled in a PhD program at UCSB's Bren School of Environmental Science and Management. Here, my work involves the application of spatial modeling techniques to the study and resolution of various environmental problems. My research advisor is Dr. Arturo Keller.

    I have received extensive academic training in the analysis of environmental phenomena that occur over space and time. Long story short: I can do math, program, make maps, solve problems, and then tell you all about how I did it in a clear and coherent voice once I have finished.

Spatially Explicit
Life Cycle Assessment

The Development and Application of Methods for the Use of Spatial Data in LCA

We propose a set of novel techniques for the practice of Spatially Explicit Life Cycle Assessment (SELCA) modeling wherein spatially explicit inventory emissions flows and impact assessment characterization factors are modeled as a set of sparse geographically referenced data grids. A case study implementation of these techniques is introduced in which spatially explicit impacts to human health are evaluated for the United States chemicals manufacturing industry as based upon available inventory emissions data from the 2010 Toxic Resource Inventory (TRI). Following the discussion of this case study's results, consideration is then given to the many technical consequences which emerge from this method’s commitment to the use of continuous spatial data and grid based spatial analytics in the context of existing LCA workflows.

Funding Agency: National Science Foundation
Date: 05.01.2011 - 05.01.2013 Learn More

Wastewater Reuse

Understanding the Combined Energy and Water Usage Efficiency of the Reuse of Treated Wastewater via Groundwater Recharge

The overarching goal of this project will be to devise a model which can be used as a planning tool to improve the joint energy-water usage efficiency of a water reuse system involving some form of groundwater recharge.

Rooftop Photovoltaics

Evaluating the Potential Electricity Generation Capacity of Rooftop Mounted PV within the State of California

Using a set of LiDAR derived rooftop solar production potential maps generated by CH2M Hill for four counties in the State of California, we will develop a regression model to predict the anticipated maximum quantity of energy that could theoretically be supplied annually by rooftop mounted solar PV installations on all of the built structures within the State of California.
