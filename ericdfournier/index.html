I'm glad you made it. My name is Eric. I am a scientist. I study the earth's capability to support human life. I chose to do this partly because I am a human - who is alive - and partly because this capability appears to be in decline. This choice seemed logical enough.

Background

I have a B.S. in environmental science from Bucknell, a M.S. in environmental science from Yale, and within the year I expect to also have completed a M.A. in geography as well as a Ph.D. in ecoinformatics from UCSB. As you might be able to tell, I have been going to school for a long time. It has been a fairly big part of my life.

Research

For the last few years I have done a considerable amount of work in the area of corridor location planning. This basically just involves devising techniques to rapidly deduce the shortest path between two points positioned within a space characterized by multiple and continuously non-uniform costs. In general though, I am pretty knowledgable when it comes to the use of geographic information software, the numerical modeling of dynamical natural systems, and big picture concepts related to human-ecosystem interactions.

Interests

I suppose like most people I do lots of different things. I've never owned a car so I need to get somewhere I usually ride my bike. If there are waves I surf. If there are no waves I will run or play soccer.
